Abstract

This paper presents a theoretical study on bridging internal probability and self-consistency for large language model (LLM) reasoning. We explore the relationship between the internal probability distributions of LLMs and their self-consistency behavior during reasoning tasks.

Overview

Large Language Models have demonstrated remarkable reasoning capabilities. This work provides a theoretical framework for understanding how internal probabilities relate to self-consistency in LLM reasoning, offering insights into improving the reliability and accuracy of LLM-based reasoning systems.
